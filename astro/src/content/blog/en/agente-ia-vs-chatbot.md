When someone says they have a chatbot on their website, they usually mean a system that follows a decision tree. If the visitor types something that matches one of the pre-set options, they get a response. If it doesn't match, the system stalls or escalates to a human.

That is useful. But it has a clear limit: it can only respond to what someone already anticipated.

## The fundamental difference

An AI agent understands natural language. It doesn't need the question to be on a predefined list. It can interpret context, remember what has been said earlier in the conversation, and generate an adapted response.

But the most important difference is not in how it responds. It's in what it can do.

A chatbot responds. An agent acts.

## What an agent can do that a chatbot cannot

An agent connected to your systems can:

- Query the real status of an order in your database
- Register lead data directly in your CRM
- Check availability on your calendar and propose an appointment
- Escalate to the human team only when the situation requires it
- Operate at 3 in the morning without supervision

All of this happens without any person intervening. And it happens using your own information, not pre-written answers.

## When an agent makes sense

Not every business needs an AI agent. If the volume of inquiries is low or the questions are always the same, a simpler system may be sufficient.

An agent makes sense when the volume is high, the inquiries are varied, or there are actions to execute as a result of those inquiries. In those cases, the return is clear and fast.
