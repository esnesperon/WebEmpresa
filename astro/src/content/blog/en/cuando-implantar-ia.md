There is a lot of noise around artificial intelligence. Every week a new tool appears that promises to transform some aspect of the business. And faced with so much stimulation, it is easy to fall into two opposite mistakes: implementing everything without criteria, or doing nothing while waiting for a better moment.

The reality is that AI makes sense in some contexts and not in others. And there are ways to identify which side you are on.

## Signs your company is ready

There are clear indicators:

**You have data.** AI learns from information. If you do not have a history of operations, conversations, documents, or records, any AI system you build will have a very limited foundation.

**You have a specific problem.** AI projects that work solve a specific need: reducing customer response time, classifying incoming documents, detecting anomalies in production data. Those that fail seek to "apply AI" without knowing exactly to what.

**There is a repetitive process with predictable decisions.** If the same type of inquiry comes in a hundred times a week and is always answered in a similar way, there is a real use case there.

## Signs it is better to wait

If processes are not documented or consistent, implementing AI on top of them only amplifies the chaos. If the team is not clear on how the business works, no automated system is going to fix that.

AI is a layer on top of processes that already work, not a substitute for processes that don't exist.

## The right starting point

Before implementing anything, the most valuable step is an audit of the process you want to improve. Understand how it works today, where time is lost, what decisions are made and with what criteria. With that information, the choice of tools and the design of the solution are much easier to get right.
