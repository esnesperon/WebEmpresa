Traditional e-commerce was designed to sell t-shirts and sneakers to end consumers (B2C). However, when companies try to force these same platforms to sell to other businesses (B2B), problems begin immediately.

**B2B isn't about "Add to cart". It's about relationships, negotiations, and approval workflows.**

## The problem with traditional E-commerce in B2B

If you use a generic platform to sell to other businesses, you will encounter structural obstacles:
- **Dynamic pricing:** In B2B, Client A might have a 15% discount based on historical volume, while Client B has fixed prices negotiated in a contract. A catalog with a single visible price doesn't work.
- **Approval workflows:** Often, the person selecting the products doesn't have the final authorization to make the payment.
- **Complex payment methods:** Net 30, 60, or 90 terms, SEPA transfers, and credit lines are the norm, not the credit card exception.

## The solution: Custom B2B E-commerce

To scale enterprise sales without clogging up the sales team, a web architecture that understands business complexity is required:

1. **Personalized client portals:** Upon logging in, each client sees *their* catalog, with *their* negotiated prices and billing history.
2. **Automatic quote generation:** Instead of "buy now", the client can build a complex order and request a quote (Quote-to-Cash). The system can approve it automatically based on predefined rules or send it to the sales team for review.
3. **Deep ERP integration:** B2B e-commerce is not an isolated system. It must be connected in real-time with the ERP (to reflect actual stock across multiple warehouses) and the CRM (so the sales rep knows what their key client is looking at).

## The return on investment is twofold

Modernizing your B2B channel not only **increases sales** by offering a frictionless experience to clients who increasingly prefer digital self-service (especially the new generation of B2B buyers). It also **drastically reduces operational costs**, freeing sales reps from "taking orders" so they can focus on high-value tasks: opening new accounts and closing strategic deals.
